About

I am a Professor of Economics at Yıldız Technical University. My research spans labor & product market imperfections, human capital accumulation, and macroeconomic growth with heterogeneous agents. I teach macroeconomics, growth theory, and mathematical statistics at undergraduate and graduate levels.

My recent work studies variable markups and efficient bargaining in overlapping generations (OLG) models and their implications for inequality, labor share, and growth.
